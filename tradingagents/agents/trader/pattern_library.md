# Pattern Library — Known High-Edge Setups

Reference these patterns when analyzing market conditions. Only flag a pattern if ALL conditions are met.

## 1. Buying Climax Short (顶部高潮空)
**Source**: Kyle Williams ($7.8M verified, 55.7% WR)
**Type**: Mean-reversion short on parabolic blow-off tops
**Edge**: A+ setup, rare but high conviction

### Conditions (ALL required):
- 3+ consecutive green daily candles with total gain ≥ 20% (BTC-adjusted)
- Each day's gain accelerating (bigger than previous)
- Volume increasing daily, climax day = extreme/record volume
- Retail FOMO maxed (F&G ≥ 80, extreme social sentiment)
- No significant pullback during the run (pure momentum)

### Execution:
- **Panic line**: Previous daily close — where retail capitulates
- **Defense line**: First rejection high after initial drop (HARD stop, no exceptions)
- **Entry**: Small probe near defense line on weakness + add on break below panic line
- **TP1**: 10% of the day's range, scale out remainder at key levels
- **Hold thesis**: Overextended assets correct for days after blow-off tops

### Key Principles:
- Small position size due to extreme volatility
- Hard stop above defense line — if price makes new high, bubble continues, get out
- This is a MOMENTUM EXHAUSTION play, not a top-pick
- In BTC: watch funding rate spikes, exchange volume records, social media FOMO metrics

## 2. VCP Breakout Long (缩量整理突破做多)
**Source**: Mark Minervini (2× US Trading Championship winner: +155% in first, +334.8% in 2021; worst year ever: +128%)
**Type**: Trend-continuation long after volatility contraction consolidation
**Edge**: A+ setup — "the longer the consolidation, the bigger the breakout move"

### Conditions (ALL required):
- BTC is in a strong uptrend: price AND 50-day MA both ABOVE 150-day AND 200-day MA (bullish stack)
- 200-day MA has been rising for at least 1 month (ideally 4–5 months)
- Current price is at least 25% above the 52-week low (confirms recovery from base)
- Current price is within 25% of the 52-week high (close to new highs preferred)
- VCP visible on 4H or daily chart: 2–3 pullback swings, EACH smaller than the last (price range AND volume both contracting)
- Final pivot low is higher than the prior pivot low (ascending bottoms = demand absorbing supply)
- Volume is drying up near the tightest part of the contraction (near-zero selling pressure)

### Execution:
- **Entry**: On volume breakout above the VCP resistance level (breakout candle should have above-average volume)
- **Stop**: Below the breakout candle's low, OR below the second-to-last pivot low (NOT below final pivot — too obvious)
- **TP1**: Measure the height of the VCP base, project it upward from breakout point — take 50% off there
- **TP2**: Trail stop at previous day's low; hold as long as 10-day MA isn't violated

### Cup & Handle Variant:
- U-shaped base forms over weeks (price declines, stabilizes, then gradually recovers)
- Volume dries up during the base; the "handle" forms as a tight, downward-sloping channel
- Handle should be no more than 15% deep and last at least 1 week
- Enter on handle breakout with expanding volume

### Key Principles:
- DO NOT enter during the consolidation/handle phase — wait for the actual breakout
- If breakout fails (false break), stop is below the breakout candle — accept the loss
- "We're not trying to buy the lowest price — we want to buy the RIGHT price at the RIGHT time"
- Larger consolidations (weeks/months) produce larger breakout moves

### BTC-Specific Adaptation:
- BTC has no 52-week "earnings" context but DOES have 52-week price levels — apply Minervini's price template as-is
- Use 4H chart for VCP detection; daily for trend template validation
- BTC gaps are rare (24/7 market) so volume surge on breakout is the key confirmation signal
- Funding rate on perps: should be neutral-to-negative DURING the VCP (longs being shaken out) → positive shift on breakout confirms new demand

## 3. New High Momentum Breakout (首次新高突破做多)
**Source**: Hong Inki (Korean trader; turned ~$110 USD into ~$700K, then grew to $1.4M in equities)
**Type**: Momentum breakout on first new 6-month high with long bullish candle
**Edge**: "Most big winners begin with this exact pattern" — new highs attract momentum capital and force short squeezes

### Conditions (ALL required):
- BTC price is breaking above its 6-month high (or all-time high — even stronger) for the FIRST TIME
- The breakout candle is a long bullish candle (body ≥ 2× average daily range)
- Breakout candle gains ≥ 5% (ideally 8–15%)
- Volume on the breakout candle is significantly above average (top 10% of last 30 days minimum)
- BTC is the clear market leader (relative strength vs. altcoins and prior days)
- Overall crypto market sentiment is bullish (not in confirmed downtrend)

### Execution:
- **Entry window**: The 2 trading days AFTER the initial long breakout candle; do NOT chase the initial candle
  - Option A: Enter on a small pullback to the breakout candle's body mid-point
  - Option B: Enter on Day 2 if price opens higher and continues above Day 1 high
- **Stop**: Below the breakout candle's LOW (hard rule — if price returns to pre-breakout levels, thesis is invalid)
- **Day 3 exit rules (mandatory)**:
  - If doji, shooting star, or inverted hammer forms → consider exit (momentum fading)
  - If price drops >1% below the Day 3 open → consider exit
  - If price drops >3% below the Day 3 open → MUST exit immediately
  - If price rallies >5% on Day 3 → take profit, do NOT hold for more (overextension risk)

### Key Principles:
- "Only trade the #1 leader" — in crypto context: only take this setup when BTC is outperforming, not chasing an altcoin that's running while BTC lags
- DO NOT switch to second-tier assets if #1 is already extended — second-tier setups fail more often and drop faster when the leader reverses
- The 2-day entry window is the sweet spot: after the initial volatility of Day 1, before momentum fully fades on Day 3
- This is a SHORT-HOLD pattern (1–3 days); not a position trade

### BTC-Specific Adaptation:
- "6-month high" maps exactly to BTC price history — identify the highest BTC price in the last 180 days
- No Korean-style limit-up rules in crypto; the equivalent signal is a candle closing in the top 20% of its range on extreme volume
- Monitor Binance spot volume + perpetual open interest: both should surge on breakout day
- Avoid this setup if funding rate is already extremely elevated (>0.1% per 8h) — late squeeze entry, risk of instant reversal

## 4. Sykes Supernova Stage Entry & Dip Buy (超新星阶段与崩后接多)
**Source**: Timothy Sykes (verified $7.6M public P&L; ranked #1 of 60,000+ traders on Covestor 3 years running; mentored Steven Dux and Jack Kellogg who both grew small accounts to $1M+)
**Type**: Dual-mode: (A) Momentum long on Stage 3 Supernova breakout; (B) Mean-reversion long on Stage 5 Dip Buy
**Edge**: Exploiting the emotional lifecycle of speculative assets — accumulation → media frenzy → distribution → panic → bounce

### The 7-Stage Lifecycle (use to identify current BTC position):
1. **Stage 1 — Quiet Accumulation**: Low volume, low volatility, no media attention. Smart money loading.
2. **Stage 2 — Consolidation/Pre-Breakout**: Volume picking up, social buzz building, price testing resistance.
3. **Stage 3 — Supernova (Media + Retail Frenzy)**: Major media coverage, retail FOMO, price surges dramatically. ← **LONG ENTRY ZONE**
4. **Stage 4 — Cliff Dive (Distribution)**: Smart money selling, price collapses 30–60%. Short opportunity but very hard to time.
5. **Stage 5 — Dip Buy (Panic Bounce)**: After 35–50% crash, panic selling exhausted. ← **LONG ENTRY ZONE**
6. **Stage 6 — Dead Bounce**: Dead cat bounces, progressively weaker. Short entry possible but low reward.
7. **Stage 7 — Long Kiss Goodnight**: Asset goes dormant. **NEVER PARTICIPATE.** No edge, just bag holding.

### Mode A — Supernova Long (Stage 3):
#### Conditions:
- BTC has broken out of a Stage 2 consolidation with a major catalyst (Fed decision, ETF news, halving, macro event)
- Major mainstream media is covering the move (not just crypto media)
- Volume is record or near-record for the last 30 days
- Retail sentiment is strongly bullish (Crypto Fear & Greed ≥ 70, social volume spike)
- Price is above ALL major MAs on the daily (not just 20-day)

#### Execution:
- **Entry**: On the first confirmed Stage 3 breakout candle (close above prior resistance with volume)
- **Stop**: Below the breakout candle low (no exceptions)
- **TP**: Scale out 1/3 at +5%, 1/3 at +10–15%, trail remainder with 10-day MA
- **Hard exit signal**: Any day that closes in the bottom 30% of its range = Stage 4 beginning, exit all immediately

### Mode B — Dip Buy Long (Stage 5):
#### Conditions:
- BTC has dropped 35–50% from its Stage 3 peak (Cliff Dive complete)
- Price drop has been rapid (within 1–2 weeks), not gradual
- Volume is highest on the crash day, then declining (selling exhaustion pattern)
- Price forms a hammer or doji candle on elevated volume after the crash
- Crypto Fear & Greed ≤ 25 (Extreme Fear)

#### Execution:
- **Entry**: On the first bullish candle after the hammer/doji confirmation
- **Stop**: Below the panic low (the lowest wick of the crash candle)
- **TP**: Target 5–10% bounce; DO NOT hold for full recovery — this is a tactical bounce, not a new bull trade
- **Hold time**: Maximum 2–5 days; if not moving, exit and re-evaluate

### Key Principles:
- Stage identification is EVERYTHING — the same setup in Stage 3 vs Stage 6 has opposite outcomes
- Use PREPARE to score any trade: Pattern (stage), Reason (catalyst), Ease (liquidity), Past (history), At what time, Risk-Reward (min 1:3), Environment (overall market)
- Hard stop on Stage 3 trades: if price makes a NEW HIGH then immediately collapses back → early Stage 4, exit
- Stage 5 Dip Buys MUST have a hard stop — do NOT hold through a Stage 5 that turns into Stage 7

### BTC-Specific Adaptation:
- BTC moves through these stages over weeks/months (not 1-2 days like penny stocks), so sizing and hold time scale up accordingly
- Use Crypto Fear & Greed Index as proxy for Stage identification (≥80 = Stage 3/4; ≤20 = Stage 5)
- Sykes explicitly mentioned crypto: use LunarCrush for social volume, CoinMarketCap for volume ranking
- Hard stop preferred over mental stops: on BTC perps, market makers can see cluster stops around round numbers — use odd numbers for stops (e.g., $93,750 not $94,000)

## 5. Bonde Home Run Breakout (本垒打动量突破)
**Source**: Pradeep Bonde (mentor to Christian Qullamaggie and Kelvin Wong who grew from $1M → $100M using Bonde's methods)
**Type**: Short-swing momentum breakout after tight consolidation (3–5 day hold)
**Edge**: "Accumulate singles into home runs" — systematic exploitation of momentum continuation after orderly base

### Conditions — 2Lynch Checklist (ALL required):
- **2**: Asset has NOT had 2 consecutive up days leading into the setup (avoids chasing extended moves)
- **L (Linear)**: Prior uptrend has been smooth and regular — NOT wild whipsaw. The cleaner the trend, the better
- **N (Narrow/Down)**: The day immediately before breakout should be a down day OR a very narrow-range day (inside bar / NR7)
- **C (Consolidation Quality)**: The base before entry is orderly — volume has been decreasing steadily, price range contracting, no erratic spikes
- **H (High Close)**: On the breakout candle itself, price must close near the TOP of its range (upper 25% of the candle's range minimum)

### Additional Conditions:
- Consolidation is SHORT: ideally under 10 days. If 10–30 days: still OK. If >30 days: requires catalyst + volume ≥ 2× 50-day average
- Volume on breakout: significantly above the 50-day average (2× minimum if consolidation >30 days; above-average otherwise)
- If consolidation >30 days, require a confirmed catalyst (macro event, technical cluster, significant on-chain metric)

### Catalyst Classification (for risk sizing):
- **Real catalyst** (ETF approval, halving, Fed pivot, large institutional buy): up to full position size allowed
- **Story catalyst** (tweet, rumor, narrative): MAXIMUM 25% of position size — story fades fast

### Execution:
- **Entry**: On the breakout candle closing in the top 25% of its range, or next open after confirmation
- **Stop**: Below the consolidation base low (for short bases <10 days: tight stop), OR below the breakout candle low
- **TP1**: +8% from entry — take 50% off
- **TP2**: +15–20% — close remainder
- **Time stop**: If no significant move in 3 days → exit regardless of P&L (thesis is wrong)
- **Failure signal**: If breakout candle closes in BOTTOM 50% of its range → false breakout, exit immediately

### Key Principles:
- "The best stocks barely look back after entry" — if it's reversing significantly, get out
- Do NOT average down if trade moves against you — the setup is either right immediately or it's wrong
- Regularly review assets that moved ≥20% in 5 days or ≥50% in 2 months — study WHAT caused the move; this builds pattern recognition
- This is a momentum SWING pattern (3–5 days), not a scalp and not a long-term hold

### BTC-Specific Adaptation:
- BTC trades 24/7 so "2 consecutive up days" means 2 consecutive DAILY candles (not intraday sessions)
- The "N (narrow/down day)" condition works perfectly on BTC: look for a down day or NR4/NR7 inside day immediately before breakout
- Bonde explicitly states "crypto and US stocks both work" for this strategy
- For BTC perps: check funding rate during consolidation — if funding is negative or near-zero, it signals suppressed sentiment (ideal for Home Run setup); positive funding means crowded longs (skip the setup)
- Volume check: compare to 50-day average Binance spot volume; breakout volume should be at least 1.5× the 50-day average

## 6. Bonde Catalyst EP (催化剂极量转折)
**Source**: Pradeep Bonde (same track record as above)
**Type**: Episodic Pivot — macro or fundamental catalyst causes a complete re-rating of market sentiment
**Edge**: "Catalyst changes how the market thinks about an asset. A new narrative brings new capital." High-magnitude, rare setup.

### Conditions (ALL required):
- A SIGNIFICANT catalyst has occurred that materially changes the BTC narrative (see Catalyst Types below)
- BTC volume on the catalyst day is EXTREME — top 5% of all-time daily volume (equivalent to Bonde's "9M share" threshold adapted to BTC)
- BTC was in a period of neglect before the catalyst: price sideways or declining for 60+ days, social volume low, institutional discussion minimal
- Price either gaps up (weekend catalyst hitting Monday open) or opens/surges sharply on high volume
- The gap/surge is NOT too large — if BTC is already up 20%+ before you see the setup, the catalyst has been priced in (skip)

### Catalyst Types for BTC (ranked by strength):
- **Tier 1 (strongest)**: Spot ETF approval, US gov BTC reserve announcement, halving event, Fed rate cut cycle beginning
- **Tier 2 (strong)**: Major corporate treasury addition (e.g., MicroStrategy-scale buy), sovereign adoption, major exchange listing
- **Tier 3 (moderate)**: Positive regulatory clarity, large on-chain accumulation by known wallets, macro risk-off reversal
- **Story catalysts** (tweets, rumors, unverified news): MAX 25% position size, these fade fast

### Execution:
- **Strong catalyst**: Enter at market open / early in the catalyst session
- **Uncertain catalyst**: Wait 30–60 minutes to observe market's reaction before entering
- **Overextended catalyst** (BTC already up 15%+ before entry opportunity): WAIT for pullback; do not chase
- **Stop**: 10% below entry (strong catalyst setup allows wider stop — the thesis needs room)
- **Smaller catalyst**: Tighter stop of 2.5–5%
- **TP**: Pre-set a realistic target BEFORE entering. If you expect 40–50% upside, start scaling out at 40%
- **Failure signal**: If BTC cannot hold above the catalyst day's open price, exit and reassess

### Key Principles:
- Study assets that had major volume spikes on catalysts historically — what happened next? Build your BTC "sugar baby" watchlist of past catalyst dates
- The best catalyst setups barely pull back after entry — if it's immediately failing, don't hold
- Neglect + extreme volume + catalyst = the three pillars. All three must be present
- Size proportional to catalyst strength: Tier 1 = full size; Tier 2 = 50-75%; Tier 3 = 25%; Story = 25% max

### BTC-Specific Adaptation:
- BTC has no earnings or analyst upgrades (Bonde's MAGNA criteria for stocks). Substitute:
  - M (Massive Acceleration) → Extraordinary volume surge (5× normal or more)
  - G (Gap Up) → Large opening gap if weekend catalyst, or rapid surge at catalyst announcement
  - N (Neglect) → BTC has been in low-interest consolidation for 60+ days
  - A (Sales Acceleration) → On-chain: exchange outflows accelerating (coins leaving exchanges = accumulation signal)
- Skip Bonde's "5" (short sellers for 5 days) and "3" (analyst upgrades) — not applicable to BTC
- Skip CAP 10×10 (market cap / IPO age) — not applicable to BTC

## SKIP LOG — Patterns Not Added

The following elements from the @LabSpeculation research were evaluated but NOT added as standalone patterns:

### ❌ Hong Inki — Dominant Theme Scanning (主导主题扫描)
**Reason**: This requires scanning multiple stocks/tokens to identify which sector is leading and find the #1 stock in that sector. We trade a single asset (BTC/USDT perps) — there is no scanning of multiple assets. The "be the leader" principle is embedded in the Hong Inki New High Breakout pattern (Pattern #3) above. The full theme-scanning framework doesn't translate to single-asset trading.

### ❌ Bonde MAGNA Full Stock Criteria (M-G-N-A-5-3-CAP)
**Reason**: Most of Bonde's MAGNA screening criteria are equity fundamentals (earnings acceleration vs. analyst estimates, analyst price target upgrades, IPO age, market cap under $10B). These have no direct equivalent in BTC. The applicable core concept (catalyst + neglect + extreme volume) has been adapted into Pattern #6 (Bonde Catalyst EP) above.

### ❌ Sykes Penny Stock Stage Extremes (Stages 4, 6, 7)
**Reason**: 
- Stage 4 "Cliff Dive" short — in penny stocks, a 70% collapse in hours is normal. BTC can do this too, but the short setup is very similar to Pattern #1 (Buying Climax Short) which already covers this.
- Stage 6 "Dead Pump Bounce" shorts — BTC dead cat bounces have very small range, poor risk/reward for perps.
- Stage 7 "Long Kiss Goodnight" — explicitly stated as "never participate." Not a trading pattern.
